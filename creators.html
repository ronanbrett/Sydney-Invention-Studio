Documentation

Black Spring reviews a post-industrialised, post-digital, post-social landscape. A scenario of doom in an artificial nature where spring has gone wrong, where the living and breathing existence of humankind has been wasted and abandoned. The tectonics of environment are formed by the unrecognisable fragments of abandoned appliances and devices.

In this scenario, life is breathed by the slow unfolding dance of a new dawn, a new spring, by the virtue of machinic life replicating, stimulating, unfolding itself. In this newly awakening nature, the human dancer finds her place in the corporeal exchange with a cloud of animated beings, a quasi-cherry-blossom carnage of a digital species that mimic her movement and interact in a choreography, inscribing a layering, a trace of movement and gestures from which new interactions grow.

Black Spring is a collaboration between computational design, interaction design, digital fabrication and body performance. The installation will continue to develop a narrative during the four weeks of the exhibition, continuously growing elements of the installation, forming and intensifying traces and behavioural patterns of performers and spectators.
